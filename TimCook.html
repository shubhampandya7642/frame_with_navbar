Tim Cook


Tim Cook is the CEO of Apple, a position he has held since 2011. Cook was born on November 1, 1960 in Robertsdale, Alabama. He joined Apple in 1998 after working at IBM for 12 years and briefly at Compaq. Before becoming CEO, Cook served as Apple's Chief Operating Officer and SVP of Operations. Cook took over as CEO in 2011 after Apple co-founder Steve Jobs fell ill with cancer

Cook has a net worth of $1.8 billion and owns about 0.021% of Apple's shares. Under Cook's leadership, Apple has become the first company to reach a $1 trillion market cap.
